# PART I — THE LORE: "THE UNSENDING"

### Chapter 0 · The universe is over

This is the part nobody tells new pilots, so read it twice:

**The universe already ended.** Long ago. Quietly. Not with fire — with forgetting.
Stars went out one by one, and when the last one felt the dark closing in, it did the
only thing a star can do that gravity cannot undo:

**it remembered.**

It compressed everything — every galaxy, every ring of light, every storm, every
drifting mote of dust — into pure numbers. **Seeds.** A whole sky folded into a single
integer, the way a forest folds into one seed you can hold in your palm.

Then it burned itself out casting them into the dark.

What you fly through every run is not space. **It is a memory being re-read.**
That is why the same seed always builds the same sky, note for note, ring for ring.
The universe isn't procedural. The universe is *remembered*.

### Chapter 1 · You are an Echo

Here is the trick of it — the thing that makes old pilots go quiet:

You believe the ghosts flying beside you are recordings of your past runs.
The truth is upside down. **They were never copies of you. You are the newest copy
of them.** Every run, the memory re-reads itself and writes one more star into the
margin: you, again, a little wiser.

That's why your Echoes *earn stardust for you*. They aren't helping you.
They're finishing something they started. You will do the same for the next you.

And that is why, when you fall, the sky doesn't stop. You watch the others fly on —
rivals, friends, past selves. **The memory doesn't need any single star. It needs the
climbing to continue.** (The game calls this spectator mode. Pilots call it *the long look*.)

### Chapter 2 · Stardust, and why you collect it

Stardust is not treasure. **Stardust is crumbs of memory** — fragments too small for
the seeds to hold in place, shaken loose every time the memory is re-read. When you
gather them, the memory becomes a little more *certain* of itself. That's what your
upgrades really are: not gear — **conviction**. A wider magnet is the memory trusting
you with more of itself.

**Photons are different.** A photon is not a crumb — it is a piece of the original
light, and there is exactly one way to earn one: **SUPERNOVA.** Burn everything
you've gathered. Give the memory back its dust. What passes through the fire —
what needed no dust to be true — comes out as permanent light. +10% brighter,
forever, every time.

Old pilots say it plainly: *dust is what you carry, light is what you are.*

### Chapter 3 · The Sigils — the parts that woke up

Most of the memory sleeps. It replays, perfect and identical, forever.

But some parts of it — small, sharp, ancient parts — **noticed they were being
replayed.** These are the **Sigils**. At certain heights (▲10, ▲24, ▲40, then every
25) they surface and make you an offer: three pacts, always the same three for
anyone reading that seed, because *they* are part of the memory too. They cannot
cheat. They can only tempt.

Every gift has a price. GLASS STAR doubles your dust and removes your rescues —
because a star that refuses to fall gathers truth faster. ECHO BLOOM feeds your past
selves at your expense. PHOENIX FEATHER buys you one more chance and narrows
your perfection, because second chances always do.

You may PASS. The Sigils respect that most of all. *Flying pure* is the oldest pact
there is.

### Chapter 4 · The four cradle galaxies

The first four skies are handcrafted — the parts of the memory the last star polished
by hand before the end, because it could not bear to compress them lossy:

- **LUMEN** — *Where every star is born.* The first page. Gentle, golden, patient.
- **EMBER NEBULA** — *Burning skies, richer dust.* The memory of youth — everything
  faster, everything worth more.
- **FROST VEIL** — *Fast, icy, unforgiving.* The memory of loss. Nothing here waits for you.
- **VOID BLOOM** — *The edge of everything.* The last thing the star saw clearly.

Beyond VOID BLOOM begins **uncharted space** — the compressed regions, sector after
sector, forever. Out there the memory is *lossy*, and lossy memory misbehaves…

### Chapter 5 · The Mutations — where memory frays

Far from the cradle, each galaxy replays slightly *wrong* — one rule bent, permanently,
the same bend for every pilot who ever enters it. Pilots call these **Mutations**:

skies where rings drift like they're dreaming (UNSTABLE ORBITS) · skies that storm
because the memory of weather got tangled (STORMLANDS) · skies compressed so hard
the rings shrank (DWARF STARS) · skies littered with the still-beating hearts of dead
pulsars (PULSAR FIELD) · skies replayed slow, like a song at the wrong speed
(SLOW NEBULA) · skies spun too fast to ever calm down (HYPERSPIN).

A mutation is not a bug in the world. **It is a scar.** Learn the scars. Choose your sky.

### Chapter 6 · The Twelve Lights

Every twelfth galaxy, the deep space suddenly goes **white-gold and calm.** No
mutation. No trick. A gate.

These are **the Lights** — the twelve moments the dying star refused to compress,
kept whole at full fidelity no matter the cost: THE FIRST LIGHT, THE SECOND, THE
THIRD… Nobody knows what moments they were. The gates don't say. Their only
inscription is the same everywhere:

> *"The end of the story — or a door."*

Reach the FIRST LIGHT (▲156 in the sky before it) and you will understand the
inscription: the story ends. **And then it doesn't.** Beyond every Light waits another
universe — another twelve skies — because a memory this deep has more than one
layer, and the climbing must continue.

And here is the quiet, enormous thing, the last line of the lore and the first clue of
the secrets: climb long enough, rank by rank — STARDUST, SPARK, COMET, NOVA,
PULSAR, QUASAR, SINGULARITY — and the final rank a pilot can earn is called
**FIRST LIGHT.**

The ladder ends where the story begins. **You are not flying toward the First Light.
You are becoming it.** The star that remembered the universe was a pilot who
finished the climb. Maybe the previous you. Maybe the next.

That is the Unsending. The universe isn't being saved. **It's being re-lit** — one
tryhard at a time.

### Epilogue · Past the Twelfth

The Lights have names up to THE TWELFTH LIGHT. After that, the gates are only
called *THE LIGHT 13, THE LIGHT 14…* — **past the twelfth, even the memory has no
names left.** Nobody has flown far enough to learn why the names stop there.

Someone will. That's the whole point of you.

# PART III — THE ULTIMATE GUIDE (beginner → hardcore → speedrunner)

The sky is procedural, so there are no walkthroughs — only **systems**. Master them
in this order.

## LEVEL 1 · BEGINNER — learn to read the memory

**The tap.** You launch tangent to your orbit, in the spin direction, at 560 px/s.
You don't aim with your finger — you aim with **timing**. Watch one full revolution
before your first tap on any new ring.

**The capture.** Enter a ring's circle and you're caught. Pass within **34% of its
center** for a **PERFECT ARC**: x2 dust, +1 combo. Aim through hearts, not edges.

**Three ways to fall:** below the screen · drifting past **max(1.5 screens, 1200 px)**
without a catch · off the sides. You carry **1 rescue** (+1 per Save Ring level);
a rescue breaks combo unless Combo Keeper procs (up to 90%).

**Read the grammar:** breathers every 8th ring (rest there, plan there) · movers
slide 26–60 px (lead your shot) · storm rings glow gold inside a cloud of 13 heavy
motes (orbit one extra revolution and let your magnet drink the whole cloud).

**Spend order:** Dust Magnet (80) → Aim Guide (60) → Save Ring (400) →
do all three daily challenges every day (▲15 / 250 dust / 3 perfects — 100+ dust each).

## LEVEL 2 · CLIMBER — the combo economy

Every capture pays `5 × (1 + 0.1·combo) × 2^(perfect) × 2^(skip) × globalMult × galaxyReward`.
**Combo is the whole economy.** At combo 20, every ring pays triple.

- **Skip-chaining** is the core scoring tech: jumping 2+ rings = **+2 combo AND x2
  dust**, stacking with perfect for **x4**. Look for stacked alignments; remember the
  sky only counts four ahead.
- **Perfect discipline**: center-thread everything, always. It pays now (x2), it pays
  weekly (+5 Champions pts each), it builds the aim you'll need at ▲100.
- **Storm farming**: a storm cloud is ~39 base value before multipliers. With magnet
  levels + GOLD TIDE it's collected in half an orbit.
- **Upgrade order now**: Combo Keeper → Save Ring → Star Sense → Echo Yield.
- **Echo investment**: your top-3 dust runs earn beside you every run (10%→70%
  yield). One deliberately fat farming run pays for weeks. Protect your best echo.
- **Supernova timing**: photons = ⌊(best−40)^0.8⌋. Don't burn at ▲50 (6 photons) —
  push to **▲65–70** (13–15 photons = +130–150% forever). Your echoes burn.
  Your skill doesn't.

## LEVEL 3 · HARDCORE — galaxies, ranks, builds, scars

**Go deeper, always.** Rewards ×1.30 per galaxy vs difficulty +0.12: if you can
survive ▲20 in the next sky, it out-earns ▲35 in this one.

**Rank like a cartographer.** Career points = Σ (best height × difficulty × 10) over
**every** galaxy. A ▲30 you never set in a x2.0 sky is 600 points you're donating to
nobody. Sweep your whole ladder weekly. Ranks: STARDUST 0 · SPARK 300 · COMET
1,000 · NOVA 2,600 · PULSAR 6,000 · QUASAR 13,000 · SINGULARITY 28,000 ·
**FIRST LIGHT 60,000** · then ✕1, ✕2… every +30,000, without end.

**Choose your pacts** (▲10/24/40, then +25 — same cards for every racer on your seed):

| Build | Pacts | When |
|---|---|---|
| **GLASS CANNON** | Glass Star + Comet Heart | x3 dust, zero net. Farming, for pilots who don't miss. |
| **THE WALL** | Phoenix + Gravity Kiss + Still Sky | Unlock pushes. Height doesn't care about dust penalties. |
| **SKIP DEMON** | Wild Arc + Comet Heart | +4-combo skips at +40% speed. Highest ceiling, highest APM. |
| **ECHO BARON** | Echo Bloom + Gold Tide | Sacrifice today to fatten your echoes. Never on record runs. |
| **FLY PURE** | Pass everything | Clean windows beat any bonus on a pure height push. |

**Bumper tech** (galaxy 5+): a bounce is a perfect reflection + x1.1 speed (cap 952)
+ 1 combo + dust — **and it restarts your drift leash.** Bank shots into double skips
are the highest-skill move in the game. Pin ladders go where straight lines can't.
0.6 s cooldown per pin.

**Shop for scars.** Every deep galaxy's mutation is permanent and public:
push unlock heights in **SLOW NEBULA** skies (slow spin = easy aim) · farm in
**DWARF STARS** (motes x1.6) if your aim is true · main **PULSAR FIELD** if you've
mastered pin ladders · take **HYPERSPIN** only if your APM is real. Scout ahead —
the selector shows the next sky's scar before you commit the climb.

## LEVEL 4 · SPEEDRUNNER — racing the memory itself

- **A seed is a track.** Same rings, motes, storms, pins, pacts, song — forever.
  Learn it like a circuit: memorize storm paydays, pre-plan skip chains, know your
  sigil heights (▲10/24/40/65/90…) and pick your build before launch.
- **The daily seed** is the world's shared race. Run it early, grind your line, then
  hunt the **coral ghosts** — the top 5 rivals of your seed, flying their real paths in
  real time. Beat their line, then beat your own; your runs publish at ▲3+.
- **Live rooms**: everyone on your world seed shares the sky live — gold pulsing
  stars with names and heights. Same seed + live room = wheel-to-wheel racing.
- **Champions math**: weekly score = Σ over galaxies of `height × difficulty × 10 +
  5 × perfects`. Depth beats width per-run (▲50 at x2.0 = 1000 > ▲70 at x1.0 = 700),
  but it **sums across galaxies** — champions sweep the ladder. And perfects are
  never wasted: 20 perfects = a free ▲10 of score.
- **The weekly ritual**: (1) daily seed early · (2) sweep a best in every unlocked sky ·
  (3) one deep push in your strongest sky · (4) one ECHO BARON run to fatten passive
  income · (5) scout next week's mutations · (6) grind the coral ghost until it's behind you.
